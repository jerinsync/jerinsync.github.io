Fake-News-Detection-Using-Different-Predictive-Models
CSE 422: Artificial Intelligence

Machine Learning Project, Spring 2023
Using a variety of prediction models, such as Naive Bayes, Decision Tree Classifier, Decision Tree Regression, and Random Forest Classifier, this project analyzes the identification of fake news. The dataset used in this study is freely accessible on Kaggle and consists of 72,134 news articles, of which 37,021 are authentic and 34,986 are fake, after removing null values. Preprocessing involved the use of RegexpTokenizer from the NLTK package to tokenize text based on specific conditions. Lemmatization was applied for normalization, reducing words to their base forms without altering context. Stopwords were removed to eliminate irrelevant terms, and the TF-IDF vectorizer assigned weights to words based on frequency, enabling text analysis for classification. Finally, the dataset was split into training and testing sets in an 80:20 ratio to evaluate model performance effectively.
